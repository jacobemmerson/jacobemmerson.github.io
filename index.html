About

I am a recent graduate from the University of Pittsburgh where I earned my B.Sc. in computer science. To promote equitable applications of language models, I leverage the reasoning capabilities of large language models to solve problems in the public sector under Professor Ryan Shi. Additionally, I help develop models for non-invasive prenatal screening at Signature Diagnostics with Professor Paul Cohen. Previously, I analyzed cultural biases in vision-language models under Professor Adriana Kovashka.

Research Interests

Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Natural Language Processing (NLP) largely encapsulate my interests, drawing inspiration from cognitive science and linguistics. Interdiscplinary research lies the foundation for much of my work and, in my mind, is critical to understanding our models and their limitations. From an AI perspective, my interests include:
  • Multimodal Learning: learning representations from multiple modalities (vision, language, audio). How can we learn grounded representations in a human-like manner? How can we distinguish between spurious correlations and valid semantic associations?
  • Ideas Generation and Reasoning: using generative models to accelerate research, determining the causality of prior work, and the capacity for reasoning — analogical reasoning in particular — in deep learning models.
  • AI for Social Good: designing models to facilitate equity-oriented goals. Can we narrow the current socioeconomic disparities with AI rather than exacerbating them?
Despite their inherent limitations and lack of interpretability, machines are, in some sense, much more malleable than the human brain. As we try to replicate human-level capabilities in these models, what can we learn about our cognition? To this end, I am interested in the behaviors exhibited by generative models and how they parallel mechanisms of human cognition.
