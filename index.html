About

I am a recent graduate from the University of Pittsburgh where I earned my B.Sc. in computer science. To promote equitable applications of language models, I leverage the reasoning capabilities of large language models to solve problems in the public sector under Professor Ryan Shi. Previously, I studied perception biases and the social salience of vision-language models under Professor Adriana Kovashka and developed statistical models for non-invasive prenatal screens at Signature Diagnostics with Professor Paul Cohen.

Research Interests

I am broadly interested in Artificial Intelligence (AI), drawing inspiration from work across cognitive science and linguistics. I believe multidiscplinary research is critical to our understanding of large AI systems and their fundamental limitations. Recently, my work has focused on three areas:
  • Perception and Multimodal Learning: learning representations from multiple modalities (vision, language, audio). How can we learn grounded representations in a human-like manner? How can we distinguish between spurious correlations and valid semantic associations?
  • Uncertainty Quantification: quantifying the confidence and uncertainty of foundation models. Humans understand uncertainty through behavioral cues which inform our responses (e.g. Hedge phrases); can we build AI systems that similarly communicate their confidence levels in ways that are interpretable and actionable?
  • Research Agents and Reasoning: accelerating research with generative models, determining the causality of prior work, the capacity for analogical reasoning in deep learning models (if method X has been applied in domain A, what can this tell us about its application in domain B?)
As AI continues to improve and exhibit increasingly human-like behaviors, can these behaviors provide us with insights about our capabilities? How do the behaviors they exhibit reflect societal beliefs?
