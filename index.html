About

I am a recent graduate from the University of Pittsburgh where I earned my B.Sc. in computer science. To promote equitable applications of language models, I leverage the reasoning of LLMs to identify domain-specific problems and propose solutions under Professor Ryan Shi. Additionally, I help develop models for non-invasive prenatal screening at Signature Diagnostics with Professor Paul Cohen. Previously, I analyzed domain biases in attention mechanisms under Professor Adriana Kovashka.

I am applying to PhD programs for the Fall '25 admissions cycle to further our understanding of our increasingly complex models and how we can develop them to facilitate human-oriented goals. Feel free to reach out with any questions relating to my previous work or current interests.

Research Interests

My research interests primarily fall under the fields of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning. With the rapid developments in both fields and a plethora of problems, I seemingly find new interests everyday. However, these topics have remained concrete over the past year:
  • Multimodal Machine Learning: Are latent representations shared between modalities transferrable and robust to domain shifts? How can we design vision-grounded concepts such that they are compositional in nature?
  • Commonsense and Reasoning: Determining causality, deductive reasoning, decomposing complex problems; How can we design neuro-symbolic architectures to become more self-supervised?
  • A.I. for Social Good: Are our models equitable? How do modern AI methods affect the global digital divide, and how can we narrow the disparities in information literacy with AI?
