About

I am a recent graduate from the University of Pittsburgh where I earned my B.Sc. in computer science. Through Pitt, I am a research assistant for Professor Adriana Kovashka and Professor Ryan Shi (separate labs). Additionally, I help develop non-invasive prenatal screening techniques at Signature Diagnostics with Professor Paul Cohen.

Starting in the Fall '24, I will be applying to PhD programs to further my understanding of our increasingly complex models and how we can develop them to facilitate human-oriented goals.

Research Interests

My research interests primarily fall under the fields of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning. With the rapid developments in both fields and a plethora of problems, I seemingly find new interests everyday. However, these topics have remained somewhat concrete over the past year:
  • Multimodal Machine Learning: Language modeling with continuous data (audio, video), in-context learning with vision-language models, visual reasoning capabilities
  • Commonsense and Reasoning: Determining causality, decomposing complex problems, self-justification for generative models
  • Domain Adaptability of Large Models: Are our models equitable? How can we combat data-driven biases? Can we leverage non-parallel out-of-domain data to mitigate culture-specific biases?
  • A.I. for Social Good: personalized education, resource allocation, detection of fraudulent content
I also like to follow progress in Quantum Machine Learning :)
